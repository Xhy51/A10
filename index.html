1. Black Hat Visualization (Misleading Histogram)

This “Black Hat” histogram intentionally distorts the data by cutting off the y-axis at 1000 counts and stretching the x-axis to 10 000 s, making normal delays look dramatic. 75% of values are squeezed into a narrow sliver, exaggerating the tail of the distribution.

2. White Hat Visualization (Accurate Box Plot)

This “White Hat” box plot corrects the distortion by using a proper 0–max axis, true quartiles, and standardized whiskers/outliers. It uses neutral styling and clear annotations to explain each component without sensationalism.
